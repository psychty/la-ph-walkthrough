Public Health Outcomes in West Sussex and local areas

This analysis shows a walkthrough of our key Public Health indicators across the lifecourse from early years to retirement and older age. Although some issues affect the population at all ages, outcomes have been placed in the life stage where the impact may be greatest.

The first section shows outcomes for upper tier local authorities in Sussex (West Sussex, East Sussex, and Brighton and Hove), and the second section shows outcomes for lower tier local authorities within these areas. Note that not all indicator outcome data are available at both upper tier and lower tier local authority levels (usually due to low counts within smaller areas). Brighton and Hove, as a unitary authority, appears in both sections of this analysis.

Each circle represents an outcome and you can hover hover and click on a circle to find out more.

The colour of the circle shows the value for the chosen area compared with England (usually based on whether the 95% confidence intervals overlap). In some cases, a low value might be better (for example the rate of teenage pregnancies) and in other cases a higher value may be better (e.g. uptake of screening) and as such a green circle denotes that the value is significantly better than the value for England and a red value denotes that the value is significantly worse than England. A statistically similar value is denoted by a yellow circle. Where it is not appropriate to that a high or low value is better, or if it is not possible to compare against the national value, the circle will be coloured grey.

You can also find the comparison by hovering over a circle.

In time, more information will be added to this analysis, including trends over time, comparisions with other areas such as regions or statistically similar areas.

Exploring indicators

You can explore an individual indicator and area. This works independently of the visualisation above.

Each of the three areas can be seen in relation to their 2019 statistically similar neighbours. These are not geographically close neighbours, but areas in England which share similar demographics as measured by The Chartered Institue of Public Finance and Accountancy (CIPFA).

The CIPFA Nearest Neighbours model seeks to measure similarity between Local Authorities by computing similarity based on variables such as population age, density, unemployment and taxbase per head, retail premises, housing benefit caseload, and characteristics of housing and mortality. Every area pair is given a score and the most similar 15 areas are identified as statistical neighbours.

These nearest neighbours are based on 2019 revisions to upper tier local authorities as published by Public Health England on the Fingertips platform.
